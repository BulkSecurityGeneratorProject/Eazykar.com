Risks of Non Filing and Non Disclosure

  • Date
    January 22, 2016
  • Posted by
    Pooja Hegde

Depending on which type of Assessee you are, you may or may not disclose your entire income.
However, remember that it is illegitimate to conceal your income and doing so can result in dire consequences.
Instead, you must consider investing in proper tax planning so that you don’t end-up in a last-minute mess.

Income Tax Notice

There is no dearth of Individuals who hold PAN cards but fail to file their annual returns. However, this may in fact result in the receipt of an Income Tax Notice, but the consequences can be managed by filing taxes late. However, filing late tax return is not one of the best practices for any Assessee to follow. The core reason to receive an Income Tax Notice is that the IT Department feels that you have evaded tax by showing higher losses and lower income. Also, presently all banking and investment related transactions are tracked based on your PAN number, which is required by the KYC norms. So don’t be surprised if you receive an Income Tax Notice because you did some high-volume Day Trading at the Stock market.
However, even during these tough times, you can always count on our EazyKar’s Services to make a detailed assessment of your Financial Records and also the Income Tax Notice served upon you. We provide a combination of quality and speed, which is nearly extinct.

IT Intimation

There is a fine line between Income Tax Notice and IT Intimations, and while the prior is more serious in nature, the latter is often, but not always meant only to inform. These are often sent out to Asessees in cases of pending liability, determination of refund, and absence of demand for refund in certain cases such as variation in losses. An IT Intimation may generally be sent out within a year from the Date of Filing.

Raid

Proper Tax planning and apt and detailed replies to the IT Notices is sure to bring down the risk of an IT Raid in the near future. In case you hold PAN and despite that have not filed your IT Returns for years or don’t have the right accounting system implemented, it’s never too late to start afresh. Also, if your Accountant has misinterpreted a statute that has resulted in litigation, you can always seek our Expert’s Opinion and start complying. However, the biggest misconception that most people have is that they would be slapped with an IT Notice for filing late tax return. That’s not true because the IT Department seldom chases those who show a positive attitude towards compliance, by filing taxes late. Also, if you receive an Income Tax Notice, an apt reply to the IT Department would do more good than harm.
